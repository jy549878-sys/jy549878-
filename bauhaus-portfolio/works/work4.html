Geometric Furniture

A minimalist furniture collection inspired by Bauhaus design principles, featuring clean lines and geometric forms.

The Geometric Furniture collection is a modern interpretation of Bauhaus design principles applied to functional furniture. Each piece is characterized by its clean lines, geometric shapes, and emphasis on functionality.

The collection includes a desk, stool, and vanity with mirror, all constructed from high-quality materials with precise joinery. The designs prioritize simplicity and functionality while maintaining visual appeal through careful attention to proportion and form.

The use of negative space and the exposed structural elements are deliberate design choices that reflect the Bauhaus philosophy of "truth to materials" and "form follows function." Each piece is designed to be both aesthetically pleasing and highly functional, suitable for modern living spaces.

Design Process

Concept Development

The design process began with extensive research into Bauhaus furniture design, particularly the works of Marcel Breuer and Ludwig Mies van der Rohe. Sketches and digital models were created to explore various geometric configurations.

Material Selection

Various materials were tested for their structural properties and aesthetic qualities. Solid oak was chosen for its durability, grain pattern, and ability to be precisely machined into the required geometric forms.

Prototyping

Scale models and full-size prototypes were created to test the ergonomics and structural integrity of each design. Adjustments were made to the dimensions and joinery methods based on these tests.

Production

The final pieces were crafted using a combination of traditional woodworking techniques and modern machinery. Each piece was carefully finished to highlight the natural beauty of the materials while maintaining the clean, minimalist aesthetic.
